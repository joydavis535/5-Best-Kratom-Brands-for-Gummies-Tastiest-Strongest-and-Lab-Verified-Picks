## How Kratom Gummies Are Made — Why It Matters for Quality

Understanding the manufacturing process helps you evaluate quality claims accurately.

**Step 1 — Extract Preparation**

Quality gummies start with properly extracted kratom alkaloids — not raw leaf powder. Extract allows for precise, consistent per-gummy dosing. Raw powder distribution through a gummy matrix is inherently inconsistent — creating dose variation within a single package.

**Step 2 — Formulation and pH Control**

Kratom alkaloids are blended into the gummy base. Critical quality variable: pH. Mitragynine is sensitive to acidic conditions. Brands that don't control formulation pH risk significant alkaloid degradation during this step — producing finished gummies with a fraction of the intended alkaloid content.

**Step 3 — Temperature Control During Setting**

The gummy mixture sets at controlled temperature. Excessive heat during this phase degrades alkaloid content. Quality manufacturers use precise temperature control throughout. Budget operations don't.

**Step 4 — Finished Product Testing**

The only meaningful quality verification step. The finished gummy — not the raw extract — is tested to confirm alkaloid content survived manufacturing. Jack Botanicals and MIT45 conduct this testing. Many brands don't.

## How to Read a Kratom Gummy Label

**Mitragynine content per piece in milligrams** — The number that tells you actual dose. If it says only "kratom extract" with no milligram figure — walk away.

**Total gummies per package cross-referenced with total milligrams** — Verify the math confirms consistent per-piece dosing. Inconsistent numbers signal manufacturing variation.

**Batch number** — Enables you to pull the specific COA for your production run. No batch number means no way to verify your product's actual lab results.

**COA QR code** — Direct link to batch lab results. Should be on every legitimate gummy package. If it's missing — the vendor doesn't want you checking.

**Extraction method** — Cold water, hot water, or ethanol. Each produces a different alkaloid profile.
